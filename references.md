## 3D City Model-Specific Metadata

### A Metadata ADE for CityGML

**Authors:** Labetski et al. (TU Delft)
**Published:** 2018, Open Geospatial Data, Software and Standards
This paper proposes an ISO 19115-compliant metadata Application Domain Extension for CityGML that addresses specific metadata needs for 3D geographical datasets. The ADE enables metadata storage for existing CityGML objects and other domain-specific ADEs, with elements later integrated into CityJSON's core. It includes metadata for feature counts, level of details, XLinks, external references, and 3D-specific attributes. [tandfonline](https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/abs/10.1080/17508975.2025.2582523)

### Assessing and Benchmarking 3D City Models (3D City Index)

**Authors:** Biljecki et al.
**Published:** 2023, International Journal of Geographical Information Science
**Cited by:** Multiple recent publications
This paper establishes a comprehensive 4-category framework with 47 criteria for evaluating 3D city models. It emphasizes metadata availability as a critical criterion for assessing data quality, alongside timeliness, update frequency, and version management. The framework evaluates 40 authoritative 3D city datasets globally. [filipbiljecki](https://filipbiljecki.com/publications/2023_ijgis_3d_city_index.pdf)

### CityGML Application Domain Extension (ADE): Overview of Developments

**Authors:** Biljecki et al.
**Published:** 2018
**Cited by:** 172+
This highly-cited paper provides a comprehensive overview of CityGML's ADE mechanism, which is fundamental for extending metadata capabilities beyond the core standard. [research.tudelft](https://research.tudelft.nl/en/publications/citygml-application-domain-extension-ade-overview-of-developments/)

## Geospatial Metadata Standards and Frameworks

### ISO 19115-1:2014 Geographic Information — Metadata

**Standard:** International Organization for Standardization
This is the foundational standard defining schema for describing geographic information and services through metadata. It specifies mandatory and conditional metadata sections, minimum metadata requirements for data discovery and fitness-for-use assessment, and optional elements for extensive resource description. [iso](https://www.iso.org/standard/53798.html)

### ISO 19115 Geographic Metadata Standard - Implementation Requirements and Guidance (NASA)

**Organization:** NASA Earth Science Division
**Approved:** July 2018
NASA's implementation guidelines for ISO 19115 provide standardized metadata requirements for science data products to ensure consistency across subsystems and data interoperability. This document is maintained by the ESDIS Standards Coordination Office under configuration control. [earthdata.nasa](https://www.earthdata.nasa.gov/about/esdis/esco/standards-practices/iso-19115-geographic-metadata-information)

### Content Standards for Digital Geospatial Metadata (FGDC CSDGM)

**Organization:** Federal Geographic Data Committee
This standard addresses data accuracy, completeness, timeliness, reliability, accessibility, security, and liability. It defines metadata characteristics based on four key roles: availability (determining existing datasets), fitness for use (quality assessment), data access, and data transfer. [ngs.noaa](https://www.ngs.noaa.gov/PUBS_LIB/Geospatial_MetadataStandardsForGeodeticData.pdf)

## Recent Research on 3D City Model Data Management

### Constructing a Digital City on a Web-3D Platform

**Authors:** Seto et al.
**Published:** 2020, ACM
**Cited by:** 41+
This paper addresses simultaneous and consistent generation of metadata and tile data from multi-source raw datasets for web-3D platforms, which is critical for large-scale 3D city model deployment. [dl.acm](https://dl.acm.org/doi/10.1145/3423455.3430316)

### Reviewing Open Data Semantic 3D City Models

**Published:** 2024, ISPRS Archives
This recent review identifies 27 potential dataset collections with more than 215 million building models readily available. It serves as a benchmark reference for 3D city model reconstruction research. [isprs-archives.copernicus](https://isprs-archives.copernicus.org/articles/XLVIII-4-2024/493/2024/isprs-archives-XLVIII-4-2024-493-2024.pdf)

### Development and Testing of the CityJSON Specifications

**Published:** 2022, ISPRS Archives
This paper documents the development and validation of CityJSON specifications, including metadata handling improvements over CityGML. [isprs-archives.copernicus](https://isprs-archives.copernicus.org/articles/XLVIII-4-W4-2022/169/2022/)

## Specialized Domain Papers

### Geospatial Metadata Standards for Geodetic Data (NOAA)

This publication addresses metadata needs for geodetic data within the National Spatial Data Infrastructure (NSDI), focusing on accuracy standards, quality components, and data sharing partnerships. [ngs.noaa](https://www.ngs.noaa.gov/PUBS_LIB/Geospatial_MetadataStandardsForGeodeticData.pdf)

### OGC Benefits of Representing Spatial Data Using Semantic and Graph Technologies

**Published:** OGC White Paper 19-078r1
This paper outlines the benefits of semantic representations for geospatial data and discusses geometry metadata requirements including accuracy, author, resolution, coordinate systems, and provenance information necessary for collaborative reuse. [docs.ogc](https://docs.ogc.org/wp/19-078r1/19-078r1.html)

These papers collectively represent the most influential and highly-cited works in metadata for 3D city models and geospatial data, covering both theoretical frameworks and practical implementations.
